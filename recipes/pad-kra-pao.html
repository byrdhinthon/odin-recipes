Thai Holy Basil Chicken (Pad Kra Pao)

Homepage

This classic Thai street food dish is a flavorful explosion of spicy, savory, and aromatic notes. It's incredibly quick to make and high in protein, perfect for a satisfying meal to power you through the day.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground chicken (or thinly sliced chicken breast/thigh)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-5 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 cup fresh holy basil leaves (Thai: Krapow)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • 2 large eggs (optional, for frying)

Steps

  1. Prep the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar until well combined; set aside.
  2. Saut√© Aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the minced garlic and chilies, and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant (be careful not to burn the garlic).
  3. Cook the Chicken: Add the chicken to the wok. Stir-fry, breaking up any large chunks with a spatula, until the chicken is mostly cooked through and starting to brown.
  4. Season: Pour the prepared sauce mixture over the chicken. Continue to stir-fry for 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce coats the meat and the chicken is fully cooked.
  5. Add Basil: Turn off the heat and immediately toss in the fresh holy basil leaves. Stir quickly for a few seconds until the leaves are just wilted from the residual heat.
  6. Assemble: Divide the cooked jasmine rice into bowls. Top with a generous portion of the basil chicken. If desired, fry an egg in a separate pan until the edges are crispy and the yolk is still runny, and place it on top of each bowl.
